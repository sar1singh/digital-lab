## Decisions Already Made

### Decision 1

Do not optimize for engineering showcase, portfolio projects, or resume projects. These are allowed only as secondary benefits.

Revenue is primary.

### Decision 2

Do not start with SaaS.

Reason: SaaS introduces development overhead, support burden, customer acquisition complexity, and delayed validation.

Current path:
Template -> Utility -> Toolkit -> Tool -> SaaS

No skipping stages.

### Decision 3

Do not start with newsletters, SEO sites, KDP publishing, AI ebooks, content creator businesses, or audience-first strategies.

Reason: long feedback loops and lower first-revenue probability.

### Decision 4

Avoid businesses requiring phone calls, meetings, consulting, or heavy outreach.

Preferred: automatic purchases.

## Research Summary

Research areas completed:
- Etsy
- Gumroad
- Creative Market
- KDP
- Career market
- Freelancer market
- Small business market
- Teacher market
- Wedding market
- Pet market
- Hidden markets
- Digital product markets

Research conclusion: buyers spend money when ROI is obvious, time is saved, mistakes are reduced, or operations become easier.

Strongest signal: operational utilities.

Examples:
- Calculators
- Trackers
- Dashboards
- Spreadsheets
- Finance tools
- Pricing tools

Customers buy "help me save time or make money," not "help me learn."

## Market Conclusions

Initially investigated:
- Career / job switch
- Freelancer operations
- Small business operations
- Agency operations
- Pet care
- Wedding planning
- Teacher operations

Career market is deprioritized because it has many free alternatives, lower purchase urgency, and lower first-revenue probability.

Strategic shift:
- Old thinking: Market -> Product
- New thinking: Buyer problem -> Product -> Distribution

Distribution should not dictate product decisions.

## Platform Notes

Etsy research was useful, but Etsy is not currently the primary launch platform.

Reason: India onboarding friction, including GST considerations, Payoneer, Etsy fees, and setup complexity.

Possible future platform, but not required for first validation.

Phase 1 distribution:
- Gumroad
- Payhip

Reason: fast launch, low friction, no Etsy dependency, no GST rabbit hole, and no website required.

Phase 2:
- After first sale or 5 products published, build a simple catalog website.

Phase 3:
- Evaluate Etsy, Creative Market, SEO, and own store after validation.

## Product Factory Rules

Build many small products, not one large product.

Product pattern:
1. Build Product 1
2. Publish
3. Build Product 2
4. Publish
5. Build Product 3
6. Publish
7. Observe views, downloads, purchases, and feedback
8. Adjust

Products should be:
- Small
- Focused
- Useful
- Easy to explain
- Creatable in 1-2 days

Avoid:
- Huge dashboards
- Giant Notion systems
- SaaS MVPs
- Complex onboarding
